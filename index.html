At The Scene

Do these in order
  • Stop. Check injuries first, damage second.
  • Call 911 if anyone is hurt or damage looks significant.
  • Photograph vehicles, plates, road, signals, injuries.
  • Exchange name, phone, license #, plate, insurance.
  • Get witness names and numbers before they leave.
  • Get officer name, badge #, and report number.
  • Say only what's necessary. No apologies, no guesses.
  • See a doctor within 24–72 hours, even if you feel fine.

Is this a substitute for hiring a lawyer?

No. It's built to help you make good decisions in the hours and days after a crash, and to help you recognize the specific situations — disputed fault, a government vehicle, a real injury — where calling a lawyer matters. Section 8 walks through exactly when that line is.

Does this apply if my accident already happened?

Yes — the deadlines and insurance sections apply just as much after a crash as before one. Section 6 in particular is worth reading immediately if your accident happened recently.

What if I'm not in Illinois?

This guide is written specifically around Illinois statutes — comparative negligence, insurance minimums, and filing deadlines — so it won't translate directly to another state's law.
